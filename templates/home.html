OIL PAINTING

Oil painting is a technique that uses pigments mixed with a drying oil (usually linseed oil) to create art. It is known for its rich, vibrant colors, and the slow drying time allows for blending and layering. Example: "Starry Night" by Vincent van Gogh.

WATERCOLOR PAINTING

Watercolor painting is characterized by its transparent and delicate quality. Artists use watercolor pigments, typically in the form of small tubes or pans, mixed with water to create their artworks.

ABSTRACT PAINTING

Abstract painting is a dynamic and expressive form of art that liberates artists from the confines of representing recognizable objects. Instead, it harnesses the power of color, shape, form, and texture to convey emotions, ideas, and sensations.

ACRYLIC PAINTING

Acrylic Painting has become extremely popular today with artists and crafts men and women all over the world. No longer do we have to worry about odors and allergies to solvents, so common to oil painting.

CUBIST PAINTING

Cubist painting, born in the early 20th century with artists like Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque, revolutionized the art world by deconstructing objects and subjects into geometric shapes and fragmented forms. This innovative style presents multiple perspectives within a single artwork, challenging traditional notions of representation and perception.

SURREALISTIC PAINTING

Surrealist painting, a prominent movement in the 20th century led by artists like Salvador Dalí and René Magritte, explores the world of dreams and the subconscious mind. Surrealist artworks often feature bizarre, dreamlike, and fantastical imagery, creating a sense of mystery and irrationality.

REALISTIC PAINTING

Realistic painting, a traditional art form that has evolved over centuries, strives to depict subjects with meticulous attention to detail and accuracy, aiming to capture a faithful representation of the real world. Realistic painters use techniques like shading, perspective, and fine brushwork to create images that closely resemble the subjects they depict, whether it's a portrait, still life, or landscape.

IMPRESSIONIST PAINTING

Impressionist painting, pioneered in the late 19th century by artists like Claude Monet and Pierre-Auguste Renoir, is renowned for its focus on capturing the fleeting effects of light, color, and atmosphere in a scene. This movement aimed to convey the immediate sensory experience of a moment rather than providing a precise representation.
